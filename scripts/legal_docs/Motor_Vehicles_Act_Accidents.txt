The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 is the primary legislation regulating road transport in India.

Key Sections for Accidents:
- Section 134: Duty of driver in case of accident and injury to a person.
- Section 161: Compensation in case of hit and run motor accidents.
- Section 166: Application for compensation to the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT).
- Section 184: Punishment for dangerous driving.
- Section 185: Punishment for driving by a drunken person or by a person under the influence of drugs.

Related IPC/BNS Sections:
- IPC Section 279 (BNS Section 281): Rash driving or riding on a public way.
- IPC Section 304A (BNS Section 106): Causing death by negligence.
- IPC Section 337 (BNS Section 125A): Causing hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others.
- IPC Section 338 (BNS Section 125B): Causing grievous hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others.

Procedure in case of accident:
1. File an FIR (First Information Report) at the nearest police station.
2. Inform the insurance company.
3. If there is injury or death, a claim can be filed in the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT).
4. Compensation is determined based on age, income, and extent of injury/loss.
