The Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 is an Act of the Parliament of India enacted in 1955. It applies to Hindus, Jains, Sikhs, and Buddhists.

Structure:
- Sections: 30
- Chapters: 6

Key Provisions:
- Section 5: Conditions for a Hindu Marriage (Monogamy, Sound mind, Age, Sapinda relationship).
- Section 9: Restitution of conjugal rights.
- Section 10: Judicial separation.
- Section 11: Void marriages.
- Section 12: Voidable marriages.
- Section 13: Divorce (Grounds like Adultery, Cruelty, Desertion, Conversion, Insanity, Leprosy, Venereal disease, Renunciation of world, Presumption of death).
- Section 13B: Divorce by mutual consent.
- Section 24: Maintenance pendente lite (Maintenance during the pendency of the suit).
- Section 25: Permanent alimony and maintenance.
- Section 26: Custody of children.

Grounds for Divorce (Section 13):
1. Adultery
2. Cruelty
3. Desertion (at least 2 years)
4. Conversion to another religion
5. Incurably of unsound mind
6. Leprosy (virulent and incurable)
7. Venereal disease (communicable form)
8. Renunciation of the world (sanyas)
9. Presumed dead (7 years absence)
10. Failure to resume cohabitation after decree of judicial separation (1 year)
11. Failure to comply with decree of restitution of conjugal rights (1 year)
